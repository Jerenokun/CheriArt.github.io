Frequesntly Asked Questions
Question: What happens if an artist fails to complete an artwork?
Unfortunately, he/she will have to pay for all the materials used. But this depends on how long the artist is taking to create the artwork. If the artwork is taking more than a month, then that is the time when the artist will have to pay for the art materials they used. However, if they have a reasonable answer on why they were taking so long (e.g. the canvas is large, they're busy with their work or school), then that we will just let them continue they were work.
Question: How will we know if you donated the money that was from our artworks?
You can join us in donating the money. That way, you can see with your own eyes that the money you worked so hard for is actually being put into a good use.
Question: Do you have any health insurance if some of us, someone got into an accident in your establishment?
Yes, we have health insurance. However, we haven't had any accidents for the past 2 years, so we will be decreasing the fundings for our health insurance over time. Also, accidents are unlikely to happen as our staff members are very careful and we try our best to remove all the toxic materials and maintain a clean working enviroment for our artists (Everyday, our janitors do their best in cleaning our floors and we also have air ventilation to lessen the smell of the paints).
Question: How do you apply for the art lessonss?
You can visit our reception and register for the classes there. Or if you have the courange/you could find the tutor, then apply directly to them. (You got this! Believe in yourself and muster up all the courage you have!)
